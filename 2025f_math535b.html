- Course Description -

This is a graduate course on differential geometry, with a focus on Riemannian geometry and geometric analysis. It covers basic concepts in Riemannian geometry (Riemannian metrics, isometries, connections and geodesics), along with advanced topics including

  • minimal surface theory.
  • metrics of positive curvature and Ricci flow.
  • systolic geometry and Gromov's systolic inequality.
  • convex integration and Nash's embedding theorem.
  • basic of symplectic manifolds.
  • basic of (almost-)complex manifolds.
  • convex integration and Nash's embedding theorem.
  • Kahler geometry and the Calabi-Yau theorem.

Students should have a strong background in real analysis (525a), differential topology (535a) and algebraic topology (540). The basics of PDEs (555a) and functional analysis is also helpful.
